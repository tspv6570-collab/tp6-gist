THE SILENT RUNNER — Film Review & Guided Discovery Tasks

A Film Review

"The Silent Runner" is a quietly powerful drama set in a remote mountain village in northern Norway. Directed by a debut filmmaker of remarkable restraint, the film follows Erik, a retired long-distance runner who returns to his childhood home after a decade of self-imposed exile, only to discover that the community he once knew has changed beyond recognition.

The plot unfolds slowly and deliberately. Erik reconnects with his elderly mother, his estranged sister, and a young neighbour who reminds him painfully of his younger self. There are no dramatic twists, no car chases, and no villains — only the quiet tension of unspoken grief and the gradual thaw of damaged relationships.

In my opinion, what makes this film truly compelling is its extraordinary use of landscape. The snow-covered peaks and frozen lakes are not merely a backdrop; they become a visual language for Erik's interior world. The cinematography is breathtaking, and the sparse, haunting score perfectly complements the film's contemplative pace.

The performances are uniformly excellent. The lead actor delivers a deeply restrained and utterly convincing portrayal, communicating volumes through expression and silence rather than dialogue. The supporting cast is equally strong, with the actress playing the sister giving a particularly moving performance.

However, it must be acknowledged that "The Silent Runner" is not a film for everyone. Its deliberately slow pace and minimal dialogue may frustrate viewers seeking fast-paced entertainment. Some scenes feel slightly overlong, and a tighter edit might have sharpened its emotional impact.

Overall, I would highly recommend "The Silent Runner" to anyone who appreciates thoughtful, beautifully crafted cinema. It is a film that lingers in the mind long after the credits roll — a rare and genuinely affecting experience.

★★★★☆ (4 out of 5 stars)

Section 7 — Guided Discovery Text Analysis Tasks

TASK 1a — BEFORE YOU READ (Lead-in activation — 3 minutes)

Instruction: Look at the title "The Silent Runner." In pairs, discuss the following questions:

  1. What kind of film do you think this might be? (genre)
  2. Would you want to watch it? Why / Why not?
  3. Think of a film you have seen recently. Write three adjectives to describe it.

TASK 1c — LANGUAGE FOCUS: NOTICING (Guided Discovery – Lexis — 6 minutes)

Instruction: Find and underline the following in the review:

  1. Three phrases the writer uses to give an opinion
  2. Five positive evaluative adjectives
  3. One evaluative adjective describing a weakness
  4. The phrase the writer uses to make a recommendation

Then answer these questions in pairs:

  1. Are the opinion phrases at the beginning, middle, or end of sentences?
  2. Do the evaluative adjectives come before or after the noun?
  3. Is the recommendation phrase formal or informal? How do you know?
Answer Key (Teacher's Copy):

2. Positive adjectives: compelling, breathtaking, haunting, excellent, convincing, moving, affecting (any five)
3. Weakness adjective: overlong (also accept “frustrating”)
4. Recommendation: “I would highly recommend…”

a. Opinion phrases appear at the start of sentences.
b. Evaluative adjectives appear before the noun or after linking verbs.
c. Formal — signalled by full verb forms and sophisticated vocabulary.
